Threats to Turtles

Pollution

Pollution, such as plastic debris and oil spills, poses a significant threat to turtles. They can mistake plastic for food, leading to ingestion and digestive issues.

Habitat Loss

Turtles face habitat loss due to coastal development, beach erosion, and destruction of nesting sites. It disrupts their nesting and feeding grounds, impacting their survival.

Illegal Trade

Turtles are often targeted for illegal trade, driven by demand for their shells, meat, and eggs. This practice severely depletes turtle populations worldwide.

Climate Change

Rising temperatures and sea levels, as well as changes in ocean currents, affect turtle habitats and nesting sites. Climate change poses a significant threat to their survival.

Bycatch

Turtles often get caught in fishing nets intended for other marine species. Bycatch is a major threat, causing injury or death to many turtles each year.

Light Pollution

Bright lights from coastal development can disorient hatchlings, leading them away from the ocean. Light pollution poses a significant threat during nesting and hatching seasons.
